Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats are supported?
Our OCR tool supports JPG/JPEG, PNG, WEBP, and BMP formats. For best results, use high-resolution images with clear, high-contrast text.
Is my image data private?
Yes, 100%. All OCR processing is done locally in your browser using Tesseract.js. Your images are never uploaded to any server. Nothing leaves your device.
Why is my OCR result inaccurate?
OCR accuracy depends on image quality. Make sure the image is sharp and well-lit, text is not skewed, and the language is correctly selected. Handwritten text may also give lower accuracy.
Can I extract text from scanned PDFs?
For PDFs, first convert them to images using our PDF → JPG tool, then run OCR on the resulting images.
How many images can I process at once?
You can upload up to 20 images in a single session. They will be processed sequentially with a progress indicator for each one.

OCR — Image to Text — Complete Guide

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) extracts text from images, scanned documents, screenshots, and photos. It converts non-searchable image-based text into editable, copyable digital text that can be used in any application.

📄 Digitize paper documents

Scan physical documents and extract their text content to create searchable, editable digital files — eliminating manual retyping entirely.

📸 Extract text from photos

Copy text from photos of signs, menus, business cards, or whiteboards without typing it manually — OCR reads it accurately in seconds.

🔍 Make PDFs searchable

Scanned PDFs contain image-based pages that can't be searched. OCR converts them to searchable text, making content instantly findable.

🌐 Multi-language support

Modern OCR supports dozens of languages including Hindi, Arabic, Chinese, and Latin-script languages — handling multilingual documents accurately.

OCR technology uses neural networks trained on millions of text samples to recognize letter shapes, words, and document layouts in images. Modern OCR (like Tesseract and Google Vision AI) achieves 99%+ accuracy on clean printed text and handles handwriting, mixed fonts, and complex layouts with high reliability across multiple languages.
