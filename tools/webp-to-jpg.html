Frequently Asked Questions

Is this WEBP to JPG converter really free?
Yes, completely free — no hidden fees, no watermarks, no account required. Convert as many images as you need.
Why does WEBP to JPG need a background color option?
WebP supports alpha transparency (like PNG). JPG does not support transparency — transparent pixels must be filled with a solid color. White is the default, but you can choose any color to match your use case.
What JPG quality should I use?
For most photos, 85–92 gives an excellent balance of quality and file size. Use 95–100 for print-quality output. Use 60–75 for web images where smaller file size matters more.
Are my images uploaded to a server?
No. All conversion is done entirely in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. Your images never leave your device — complete privacy guaranteed.
Can I convert multiple WEBP files at once?
Yes! Select as many WEBP files as you like. After conversion you can download them individually or as a single ZIP archive with one click.
Will the image dimensions change?
By default, output dimensions match the original. Use the "Max Width" setting to cap the width (height scales proportionally). This is useful for reducing large images for web use.

WebP to JPG Converter — Complete Guide

WebP is a modern image format primarily used on websites, but it's not universally supported by all image editors and older software. Converting WebP to JPG ensures compatibility everywhere — from Photoshop to photo printing services.

🖼️ Edit in Photoshop

Older versions of Photoshop and many image editors don't support WebP. Convert to JPG to open and edit web-downloaded images in any software.

🖨️ Print services

Photo printing services require JPG or TIFF. Convert WebP images before ordering prints to avoid compatibility errors at checkout.

📱 WhatsApp & apps

Some messaging apps and social platforms handle JPG better than WebP. Convert before sharing to ensure images display correctly for all recipients.

📁 Long-term storage

JPG has decades of proven compatibility. Store important images as JPG for guaranteed future accessibility as software and format support evolves.

WebP was created by Google in 2010 for web optimization and is now broadly supported in modern browsers. However, many desktop applications, older devices, and non-Google software still lack native WebP support. JPG remains the universal standard for photographic images, supported by every device and application without exception.
