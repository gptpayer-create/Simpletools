Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMR and TDEE?
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is calories burned at complete rest — just keeping organs running. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) multiplies BMR by your activity level to get actual daily calorie needs. TDEE is the number to use for diet planning.
Which BMR formula is most accurate?
Mifflin-St Jeor (1990) is consistently the most accurate for most people. Harris-Benedict (1919) tends to overestimate by 5%. Katch-McArdle is most accurate if you know your body fat percentage, as it accounts for lean mass.
How much calorie deficit is safe?
500 kcal/day deficit is safe and sustainable — it leads to ~0.5kg weight loss per week. 1000 kcal/day is the maximum recommended, yielding ~1kg/week. Going beyond this risks muscle loss, nutrient deficiencies, and metabolic adaptation.
How much protein should I eat per day?
For muscle maintenance: 0.8g per kg body weight (minimum). For muscle gain or fat loss: 1.6–2.2g per kg body weight. Athletes may need up to 2.5g/kg. Higher protein also increases satiety, helping with calorie control.
