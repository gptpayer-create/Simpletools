Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a fair coin?
Yes. Each flip uses Math.random() which returns a value between 0 and 1 with uniform distribution. Below 0.5=tails, above 0.5=heads. Exactly 50/50.
Why am I getting many heads in a row?
This is normal! In probability, streaks happen frequently. Each flip is independent — a streak of 10 heads doesn't make the 11th flip more likely to be tails (gambler's fallacy).
How many coins can I flip at once?
The Multi Coin Flip supports up to 1,000 coins at once. All results are shown as colored dots — gold for heads, dark for tails.

Coin Flip — Complete Guide

A virtual coin flip gives you an instant, unbiased heads-or-tails result using a cryptographically random number generator. It's perfect for making quick decisions, settling disputes, or playing games when no physical coin is available.

🎯 Truly random

Unlike physical coins that can be biased by slight imperfections, our digital coin flip uses a truly random algorithm for a perfectly fair 50/50 result every time.

🤝 Settle decisions fast

Use a coin flip to quickly decide who goes first in a game, who picks the restaurant, or who handles an assignment — no arguments needed.

🎲 Game mechanic

Many board games, RPGs, and classroom activities use coin flips as a game mechanic. Use this tool when you don't have a coin handy.

📊 Statistics experiments

Flip the coin hundreds of times to study probability distribution and verify the law of large numbers in statistics and math classes.

The 50/50 probability of a coin flip has been used for centuries to make fair, unbiased decisions.
