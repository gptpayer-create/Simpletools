Frequently Asked Questions

Which image formats are supported?
You can upload JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, BMP, and TIFF images. Output formats are JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP.
Will converting to PNG make my file larger?
PNG is lossless so files are typically larger than JPG, but images retain perfect quality. WebP offers the best of both — small files with excellent quality.
Does resizing affect quality?
Scaling down is always clean. Scaling up may reduce sharpness since canvas upscaling uses bilinear interpolation.
Are my images uploaded to a server?
No. All conversion is done 100% in your browser using the Canvas API. Your images never leave your device.
How many images can I convert at once?
There's no hard limit. For best performance we suggest batches of up to 50 images depending on your device's memory.

Universal Image Converter — Convert Between Any Format — Complete Guide

Different platforms, applications, and workflows require different image formats. Our universal image converter handles all common formats — JPG, PNG, WEBP, AVIF, BMP, GIF, and TIFF — letting you convert between any combination in seconds, entirely in your browser.

💡 Convert for web optimization

WEBP files are 25-35% smaller than JPG at equivalent quality. Convert your JPG/PNG images to WEBP for faster website loading.

💡 PNG for transparency

If you need to preserve transparent areas, always convert to PNG or WEBP (with alpha). JPG does not support transparency.

💡 JPG for photos

Photographic images are best stored as JPG. It provides excellent quality at small file sizes for natural scenes and portraits.

💡 Check target compatibility

Before converting, verify that your target platform or application supports the output format you are choosing.

Image format conversion is the process of reading pixel data from one file format and writing it to another. Different formats use different compression algorithms, color models, and capabilities. Our universal converter uses the browser's built-in Canvas API along with specialized decoding libraries to handle each format's unique requirements. The conversion is performed entirely client-side, supporting batch conversion of multiple images in a single session. All converted files maintain the original image dimensions unless you specify otherwise.
