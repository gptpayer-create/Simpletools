Frequently Asked Questions
What are meta tags and why do they matter for SEO?
Meta tags are HTML elements that provide structured metadata about a web page to search engines and social media platforms. The title and description tags directly influence click-through rates in search results. While keywords meta tags are largely ignored by Google today, proper title and description tags remain crucial for SEO performance.
What's the ideal length for a meta title and description?
Google typically displays up to 60 characters for page titles and up to 160 characters for descriptions. Titles should be concise, include your primary keyword near the beginning, and be compelling enough to earn clicks. Descriptions should summarize the page clearly and include a call to action.
Do I need Open Graph tags even if I don't use social media?
Yes! Open Graph tags control how your page appears whenever anyone shares a link — on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, Slack, and many other platforms. Without them, platforms will guess at titles and images, often with poor results. Adding OG tags improves the presentation of your links everywhere.
Where do I paste the generated meta tags?
Paste all generated meta tags inside the <head></head> section of your HTML file, before the closing </head> tag. For CMS platforms like WordPress, use an SEO plugin (like Yoast or RankMath) to add custom meta tags without editing code directly.
