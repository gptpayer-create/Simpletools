Frequently Asked Questions

What is CGST, SGST, and IGST?
CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST) are levied equally on intra-state (within same state) transactions — each is 50% of total GST. IGST (Integrated GST) is charged as a single tax on inter-state transactions and collected by the Central Government.
How is GST calculated on exclusive price?
GST Amount = Base Price × (GST Rate ÷ 100). Total = Base Price + GST. Example: ₹10,000 base with 18% GST → GST = ₹1,800, Total = ₹11,800.
How to do reverse GST calculation?
Base Price = Total Price × 100 ÷ (100 + GST Rate). Example: MRP ₹11,800 with 18% GST → Base = 11800 × 100 ÷ 118 = ₹10,000.
What are the current GST slabs in India?
India has 6 main GST slabs: 0% (essential goods like food grains), 5% (basic items, transport), 12% (processed foods, textiles), 18% (most goods & services, standard rate), 28% (luxury/sin goods). Special rates of 0.25% for diamonds and 3% for gold also apply.
