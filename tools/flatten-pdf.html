Frequently Asked Questions

What does flattening a PDF actually do?
Flattening merges interactive elements — like form fields, annotations and signatures — permanently into the page content. The result is a static PDF where nothing can be changed, moved or interacted with. It looks identical but is no longer editable.
Will flattening break digital signatures?
Yes — flattening removes the cryptographic signature data while keeping the visual appearance of the signature on the page. If you need to verify digital signatures later, keep a copy of the original before flattening.
Can I flatten just form fields but keep annotations?
Yes — uncheck the Annotations option and only the form fields will be flattened. Each element type (forms, annotations, signatures, links) can be toggled independently.
Is the flattened PDF smaller in size?
Usually yes, especially for heavily annotated or form-heavy PDFs, because the form data structures are removed. Enable the "Optimize output size" option for additional compression of streams.
Is my PDF sent to a server?
No. All processing happens entirely in your browser using PDF-lib. Your document never leaves your device.

Flatten PDF — Complete Guide

PDF flattening merges all interactive elements — form fields, annotations, comments, and signature layers — into a single static layer. It ensures the PDF looks identical on every viewer and prevents forms from being edited after submission.

📋 Lock form data

After filling out a PDF form, flatten it to merge your responses into the document permanently — preventing accidental edits during transmission.

🖨️ Printer compatibility

Some printers and RIP systems struggle with layered PDFs. Flattening ensures consistent print output by removing interactive elements before printing.

✍️ Finalize signatures

Flatten a digitally signed PDF to embed the signature visually — making it readable even in PDF viewers that don't support digital signature layers.

📂 Archiving documents

For long-term document storage and archiving, flattened PDFs are preferred — they remain fully readable without any special software support.

Interactive PDFs contain multiple layers including form fields (AcroForms), annotations (comments, highlights), and media. Flattening collapses these into a single rendering layer — essentially taking a "screenshot" of each page and embedding it. This is irreversible, so always keep a backup of the original before flattening.
