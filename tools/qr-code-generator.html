Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QR code?
A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that can store text, URLs, and other data. It can be scanned by any smartphone camera to instantly open the encoded information.
What is error correction level?
Error correction allows QR codes to be read even if they're partially damaged or obscured. Higher levels (Q/H) make the code more robust but slightly larger. Use H if you plan to add a logo overlay.
Can I use colored QR codes?
Yes! You can customize both foreground and background colors. Ensure there's strong contrast between them for reliable scanning. Avoid light-on-light or dark-on-dark color combinations.
What size should I use for print?
For print, use the Large (512px) or XL (1024px) option. SVG format is vector-based and infinitely scalable without quality loss — ideal for large format printing.

QR Code Generator — Complete Guide

A QR (Quick Response) code generator creates scannable 2D barcodes that encode URLs, text, contact information, Wi-Fi credentials, and more. QR codes are scanned by any smartphone camera and are widely used in marketing, payments, and access control.

🌐 URL sharing

Generate a QR code for any website URL to print on business cards, flyers, and posters — letting customers scan instead of type long web addresses.

📶 Wi-Fi access

Create a QR code that automatically connects guests to your Wi-Fi network when scanned — no need to share passwords verbally or manually.

💳 Contact & vCard

Encode your business card information in a QR code so contacts can scan it and save all your details directly to their phone's address book.

💰 Payment links

Link a QR code to your UPI, PayPal, or payment page — making it easy for customers to pay by just scanning, without typing in details.

QR codes were invented by Denso Wave in Japan in 1994 for tracking automotive parts. Today they're used in billions of applications globally — from restaurant menus and event tickets to product packaging and contactless payments. QR codes can store up to 4,296 characters and include error correction that allows them to remain scannable even when partially damaged.
