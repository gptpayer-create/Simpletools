Frequently Asked Questions

Can I screenshot any website?
You can screenshot any publicly accessible website. Sites that require login, have bot-detection, or block iframe embedding may not render correctly. For the best results, use the full URL including https://.
Why use multi-device screenshots?
With 60%+ of web traffic on mobile, your site must look great on all screen sizes. Screenshots help you spot layout issues, text overflow, broken elements, and responsive design problems before your users do. They're also essential for client presentations and design reviews.
How do I download the screenshot?
Click the "Download PNG" button below any screenshot. Your browser will save the image file directly. For best results, right-click the screenshot and "Save image as…" to choose your save location.
Are screenshots cached?
Screenshots are freshly captured each time you click Capture. Some rendering services may cache images for up to 30 minutes for the same URL. Click "Recapture" to force a fresh render if you've made recent changes to your site.
Can I use these screenshots commercially?
Screenshots of your own website can be used freely for any purpose. For screenshots of third-party sites, use them only for reference, analysis, or legitimate reporting purposes. Always respect copyright and terms of service of the site being captured.
