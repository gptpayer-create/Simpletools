Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI?
EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) is the fixed monthly payment you make to repay your loan. It includes both a principal component and an interest component. Early payments are more interest-heavy; later payments are more principal-heavy.
How is EMI calculated?
EMI=P × r × (1+r)^n ÷ [(1+r)^n − 1], where P=principal, r=monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n=number of monthly instalments.
What does the amortization table show?
The amortization schedule shows every monthly payment broken into: interest paid that month, principal paid, and the remaining loan balance. This lets you track exactly how your debt reduces over time.
How can I reduce my total interest paid?
The three main ways: (1) Increase EMI amount — pays off principal faster; (2) Shorten the tenure; (3) Refinance to a lower interest rate. Use our sliders to experiment with different scenarios.
