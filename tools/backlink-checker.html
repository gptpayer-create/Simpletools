Frequently Asked Questions

What is Domain Authority (DA)?
Domain Authority is a score from 0–100 that predicts how well a website will rank in search results. Higher DA means more authority and link equity. DA is influenced by the quantity and quality of inbound links, domain age, and content quality. New sites start near 1; established authorities like Wikipedia score 90+.
What is the difference between dofollow and nofollow links?
Dofollow links pass "link juice" (ranking power) from the linking site to yours — these are the most valuable for SEO. Nofollow links include a rel="nofollow" attribute telling Google not to pass ranking credit. Both types drive traffic, but only dofollow links directly boost your domain authority.
How many backlinks do I need to rank?
It depends on your niche and competitor profiles. For low-competition keywords, 10–50 quality backlinks from relevant sites can rank you. For competitive keywords, you may need hundreds or thousands. Quality always beats quantity — one link from a DA 80 site outweighs 100 links from DA 10 sites.
Are the backlink numbers exact?
The metrics shown are estimates based on domain signals, DNS data, and web presence indicators. For precise backlink counts, we recommend combining this with tools like Ahrefs, Semrush, or Moz. Our AI analysis provides strategic insights that go beyond raw numbers.
