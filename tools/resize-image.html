Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Contain and Cover fit?
Contain keeps the whole image visible with letterboxing if needed. Cover fills the target dimensions by cropping the image. Stretch ignores aspect ratio and fills exactly.
Can I resize images larger than the original?
Yes, you can scale up. However, enlarging images beyond their original size will reduce quality since no new pixel data is created — the browser interpolates the missing detail.
Are my images uploaded to a server?
No. Everything runs in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API. No server upload, no data storage — 100% private.
What formats can I output?
You can output the same format as the input, or convert to JPEG, PNG, or WebP during the resize operation.
How many images can I resize at once?
There's no enforced limit — batch as many as your device memory allows. For best performance we recommend batches of up to 50 images.

Resize Image Online — Free Image Resizer — Complete Guide

Resizing images is one of the most frequently performed digital tasks. Whether you need a specific pixel dimension for a website, a smaller version for email attachment, or a standardized size for a form submission, our free image resizer handles it in seconds.

💡 Lock aspect ratio

Always keep the aspect ratio locked when resizing to avoid stretching or squishing the image. Enter one dimension and let the other calculate automatically.

💡 Resize for web

Web images rarely need to be wider than 1920 pixels. Resize large photos down to 1200-1920px wide for optimal web use.

💡 Email attachment sizing

For email attachments, resize to under 1000px wide and use our Image Compressor to keep the file under 1MB.

💡 Resize by percentage

Need a half-size version? Use percentage resizing (50%) rather than calculating pixel values manually.

Image resizing recalculates pixel values to fit new dimensions using interpolation algorithms. Our tool uses browser-native canvas scaling which applies bilinear interpolation — a good balance between sharpness and smoothness for general-purpose resizing. For upscaling images beyond their original resolution, consider using our AI Image Enhancer which uses advanced super-resolution algorithms. The resized image is generated client-side and downloads directly to your device.
