Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are the conversions?
Conversions use standard SI-based constants accurate to at least 10 significant figures. Temperature conversions use exact formulas. For scientific or engineering work requiring extreme precision, always verify against official standards such as NIST.
Why is temperature conversion different from other units?
Most units convert with a simple multiplication factor. Temperature requires both multiplication and addition (or subtraction) because its scales have different zero points. For example, 0°C=32°F=273.15K — none of these zeros represent the same physical state.
What data units are supported?
We support both SI (base-10: KB, MB, GB, TB) and binary (base-2: KiB, MiB, GiB, TiB) data units. 1 Kilobyte (KB)=1,000 bytes, while 1 Kibibyte (KiB)=1,024 bytes — an important distinction in computing contexts.
