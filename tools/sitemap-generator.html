Frequently Asked Questions

What is an XML sitemap?
An XML sitemap is a file that lists all the important pages of your website, helping search engines discover and crawl your content more efficiently. While Google can find pages without a sitemap, having one ensures all your pages are known to crawlers, especially new or deep pages that might not be linked externally.
How many URLs can I add to a sitemap?
The standard allows up to 50,000 URLs per sitemap file, with a maximum uncompressed size of 50MB. If your site has more URLs, you need a sitemap index file that references multiple sitemap files. For most small to medium websites, a single sitemap file is more than enough.
What does priority mean in a sitemap?
Priority is a value from 0.1 to 1.0 that indicates the relative importance of a URL compared to other URLs on your site. It does NOT affect ranking in search results. It only tells crawlers which pages to prioritize when crawling budget is limited. Google's own guidance notes priority is often ignored.
Where should I upload my sitemap?
Upload sitemap.xml to your website root: https://example.com/sitemap.xml. Then submit it in Google Search Console under Sitemaps, and reference it in your robots.txt file with a "Sitemap:" directive. This ensures maximum discoverability across all major search engines.
