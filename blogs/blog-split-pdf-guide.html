How to Split a PDF — Extract Pages and Ranges Easily

By MD Sahil·April 17, 2026·4 min read

When Do You Need to Split a PDF?

PDF files grow large when multiple documents are merged together. Common situations where splitting is essential: receiving a 50-page report and needing only the financial appendix; extracting a single form from a packet of government documents; separating a textbook chapter to share with a study group; or isolating specific invoices from a monthly billing statement.

How to Split a PDF with GPTPayer

Our Split PDF tool handles everything in your browser:

  1. Open the Split PDF tool and upload your PDF file.
  2. Choose your split method: split all pages, extract a range, or extract specific pages.
  3. Click Split and download the output as individual files or a ZIP archive.
💡 Tip: Use our PDF Page Counter first if you are unsure of the total number of pages in your document.

How to Remove a Page from a PDF

If you want to remove a specific page rather than extract a range, use our Delete PDF Pages tool. It lets you select individual pages to remove and downloads the cleaned-up PDF instantly.

Privacy When Splitting PDFs

Since our tool runs entirely in your browser, your PDF data never travels over the internet to a server. This is especially important for legal, medical, and financial documents that you would never want to upload to a third-party service.
